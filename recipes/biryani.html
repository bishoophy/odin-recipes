Biryani

Description

Biryani is a famous mughlai delicacy mostly consumed in the countries India, Pakistan, and Bangladesh. The combination of rice and meat (mutton, chicken, etc. ) is the essence of the dish.

The dish is cooked in largely two different types. Depending on the region, the ingredients also vary to an extent. We are going to describe the Kolkata biryani.

Ingredients

  • Rice 500 g
  • Mutton 500 g
  • Potato four large
  • Yogurt 200 g
  • Onions finely chopped 200 g
  • Turmeric 2 tbsp
  • Ginger and garlic 25 g each paste
  • Red chilli powder 5 g
  • Green chilli 6 to 8 paste
  • Salt 4 tbsp
  • Spice mixture (cumin, cardamom, cinnamon, cloves, etc.)
  • Cooking oil or ghee 100 g

Steps

  1. Marinade the mutton with salt, yogurt, turmeric powder, gigner and garlic paste, green chilli paste, and spice mixture ideally for six hours.
  2. Heat the oil (or ghee) in a pan and add the potatoes. Seperate them when fried.
  3. Fry the onions until they turn golden brown. Seperate the fried onions when done.
  4. Add the mutton in the remaining oil and cook until the oil seperates from the spices.
  5. Boil water in a pot and add 2 tbsp of salt. Then add the rice. Wait till the rice is semi cooked.
  6. When the mutton becomes a little soft, add the potatoes and put the rice on the top. Cover the pot and let it cook in low flame.
  7. Tunr the flame off after 30 minutes and le the pot rest for another 15 minutes.
  8. The mutton biryani should be ready to serve.
